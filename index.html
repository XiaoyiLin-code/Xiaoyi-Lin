Biography

I am a first-year graduate student at Wuhan University, majoring in Artificial Intelligence at the School of Computer Science, supervised by Prof. Miao Li. I completed my undergraduate studies in Computer Science at the Hongyi Honor School with a GPA of 3.87/4.00, ranking 15th out of 129 students. I have a cross-disciplinary background, having won a bronze medal in the 37th National Physics Olympiad and a provincial first prize in the National Biology Olympiad.

My research interests include Hand-Object Interaction (HOI) tracking, dexterous manipulation, reinforcement learning, and embodied AI. I focus on advancing frameworks that bridge perception, planning, and control in robotics. I have experience in developing scalable frameworks for synthesizing manipulation trajectories and implementing reinforcement learning-based tracking controllers.

I am actively seeking PhD opportunities and collaborations.
